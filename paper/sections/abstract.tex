\begin{abstract}
The standard signaling story explains separation from below: low types cannot afford to imitate high types. This paper identifies the mirror failure mode: costly signaling can reverse when the relevant cost is falsification exposure rather than production effort. Sending an ambitious signal increases current persuasive value, but it also creates a one-period exposure: today's action can be repriced tomorrow if the judgment state turns to high scrutiny.

Each type has a true-type exposure cost. One primitive foundation is that higher true types carry more self-accuracy reputation at risk under adverse repricing. The high-scrutiny transition probability is $q=\Pr(j_{t+1}=H\mid j_t=L)$. The current rent from an ambitious signal for type $\theta$ is
\[
D_\theta^L(q)=B_\theta-\beta q\mu_H\Phi_\theta.
\]
The key input is the reversed exposure-cost ranking, $\Phi_H>\Phi_L$, which can coexist with $B_H>0$.

This creates a top-down signaling reversal. Let
\[
q_{\mathrm{IC}}=\frac{B_H}{\beta\mu_H\Phi_H},
\qquad
q_{\mathrm{D1}}=\text{the D1 willingness threshold},
\qquad
q^*=\max\{q_{\mathrm{IC}},q_{\mathrm{D1}}\}.
\]
For $q>q^*$, a binary-signal pure strategy that separates is not self-confirming: the high type would withdraw from $s_A$ even on-path, and D1 then removes the high-type interpretation of off-path $s_A$.

For $q>q^*$, the paper proves that, within the binary-signal pure-strategy class, the unique D1-consistent pure closed-loop assessment is pooling on the safe signal, while the ambitious signal becomes a signaling dead-end from above. A continuous-type extension in Section~\ref{sec:polarization} describes the observable shadow as support polarization of ambitious senders, not simple mean reversion. A dynamic extension then gives a forward-invariance result under a persistence law that keeps $q_t$ above the collapse boundary: under this persistence law, bounded endogenous drift does not restore separation automatically.
\end{abstract}
